About Us

We are so blessed to have you join our family and community of believers

Charis Praise Center International was founded with the goal of creating a community of believers who are passionate about worshiping God and serving Christ. We believe in the power of the Holy Spirit and strive to create an environment where people can encounter the presence of God. Our church is led by Rev Emmanuel Oduro Cosby, who has over 20 years of experience in ministry. He is committed to teaching the Word of God and helping people apply it to their daily lives

We are a vibrant and welcoming church located in the heart of the UK. Our mission is to spread the love of God and help people grow in their faith

Family Service

Our Family Service is designed to provide a welcoming and engaging environment for families to worship together. With age-appropriate teaching and activities, parents and children can grow in their faith as a unit, strengthening the bonds of the family and the church community.

Youth Ministry

Our Youth ministry is dedicated to nurturing the spiritual growth of our youngest members. Through engaging lessons, fun activities, and a safe environment, the youth and children learn about God's love and develop a strong foundation in their faith..

Outreach Ministry

The Outreach Ministry is committed to serving the local community and sharing the love of Christ with those in need. Through various initiatives, such as food drives, community service projects, and evangelistic events, we strive to be the hands and feet of Jesus in our neighborhood.

"The Lord is good to all, and his mercy is over all that he has made." (Psalm 145:9)

God's goodness is a fundamental aspect of His character, and it is manifested in His creation, His provision, and His redemptive work. As believers, we are called to reflect God's goodness in our lives, treating others with kindness, compassion, and integrity.The Apostle Paul reminds us that the fruit of the Spirit is

  • love, joy, peace,.
  • patience, kindness, goodness, .
  • faithfulness, gentleness, self-control

(Galatians 5:22-23). By allowing the Holy Spirit to work in us, we can cultivate these virtues and become conduits of God's goodness in the world.

"The Lord is merciful and gracious, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love." (Psalm 103:8)

God's mercy is His compassionate response to our brokenness and sin. It is undeserved, yet freely given. Through the sacrifice of Jesus, we have access to God's abundant mercy, which empowers us to extend that same mercy to others.

The prophet Micah declares,

  • Who is a God like you
  • pardoning iniquity and passing over transgression for the remnant of his inheritance?
  • He does not retain his anger forever, because he delights in steadfast love" (Micah 7:18).
  • As we experience God's mercy, we are called to be merciful towards others, following the example of our Heavenly Father.

"Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men." (Matthew 4:19)

Jesus calls us to follow Him, to surrender our lives to His leadership and to become His disciples. This means walking in

  • obedience to His teachings,
  • imitating His character,
  • and participating in His mission to reach the lost and make disciples.

The Apostle Peter reminds us that "Christ also suffered for you, leaving you an example, so that you might follow in his steps" (1 Peter 2:21). As we follow Jesus, we are transformed by His grace and empowered to fulfill the Great Commission, sharing the good news of salvation with the world.

"I have been crucified with Christ. It is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me. And the life I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself for me." (Galatians 2:20)

As believers, our lives are no longer our own; they have been redeemed and transformed by the power of Christ's sacrifice.

We are called to live in a manner that reflects the love, grace, and truth and to

  • surrender our lives to God,
  • allowing Him to work through us for His glory and the advancement of His Kingdom.
  • The Apostle Paul exhorts us, "I appeal to you therefore, brothers, by the mercies of God, to present your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and acceptable to God, which is your spiritual worship" (Romans 12:1).
